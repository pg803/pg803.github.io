What's the closest star?

on 1 May 2023 - Work in progress


So, What is the closest star? Most people might say Alpha Centauri, Without realizing the problem in that answer. (And to all the people saying the Sun, You're not wrong, But where's the fun?)
And to an extent, this is indeed the correct answer*.

Our stellar neighbours have a surprising lot in common with our sun, and yet are so different. The Alpha Centauri System consists of Three different stars, unlike our solar system with only one. The system consists of two sun-stars Alpha Centauri A and Alpha centauri B (With the nice names of Rigil Kentaurus and Toliman respectively) With a small, red dwarf star (Alpha Centarui C) orbiting around both, Called Proxima Centauri.

Image of Alpha Centauri A and Alpha Centauri B

Alpha centauri A and B

And these two larger stars are really similar to the sun, at 1.1 and 0.9 Solar masses. The two larger stars are close enough that they appear to the naked eye as a single star, becoming the third brightest star in the night sky (The two stars are as close as saturn is to the sun!). Also, because they are so similar and are close enough, Both stars form a binary system, and the stars orbit a Barycentre, A common point in space. (Side note: Our solar system can actually be considered a binary system. Jupiter is massive enough to make the sun 'wobble' a bit, and when jupiter and saturn are on the same side of the sun, the solar system's barycentre moves from inside the sun to outside.)

Proxima Centauri

Proxima Centauri is a small red dwarf star so faint its not visible to the naked eye. The small star orbits around the two larger stars at a distance of 0.21 Light years away, far enough that it is actually the Closest star to the sun, at 4.24 Light years.

The planets of the system

Only the small proxima centauri has any confirmed planets around it, Proxima B - an earth sized planet in the habitable zone- and Proxima D - A candidate Sub-Earth. (If you're thinking about Proxima C, a Mini-neptune, Its existance is controversial.)
