Cataclysmic Variables

on 8 May 2023 - Work in progress


Cataclysmic variables are binary star systems that consist of a white dwarf and a normal star. A white dwarf is the remnant of a star that has exhausted its nuclear fuel and collapsed into a very dense object. A normal star is a star that is still burning hydrogen in its core and shining brightly.

The white dwarf and the normal star orbit each other very closely, so close that the white dwarf can pull matter from the normal star's outer layers. This matter forms a disk around the white dwarf, called an accretion disk. The accretion disk is very hot and emits a lot of radiation.

Sometimes, the matter in the accretion disk becomes unstable and falls onto the white dwarf in a sudden burst. This causes a bright flash of light, called a nova. A nova can increase the brightness of the system by thousands of times for a few days or weeks.

Other times, the matter in the accretion disk can build up enough pressure and temperature to trigger nuclear fusion on the surface of the white dwarf. This causes an even brighter flash of light, called a supernova. A supernova can increase the brightness of the system by millions of times for a few hours or days.

Cataclysmic variables are important for astronomy because they can help us understand how stars evolve and die, how matter behaves under extreme conditions, and how the universe expands and changes over time.

For example, by measuring the brightness and distance of supernovae, astronomers can estimate how fast the universe is expanding and how much dark energy it contains. Dark energy is a mysterious force that causes the universe to accelerate its expansion.

Another example is by studying the spectra and variability of cataclysmic variables, astronomers can learn about the physical properties of white dwarfs, accretion disks, and binary stars. These properties can tell us about the origin and history of these systems and their possible future fate.

In conclusion, cataclysmic variables are fascinating and complex objects that offer us a glimpse into some of the most extreme phenomena in nature. By observing and analyzing them, we can gain valuable insights into the workings of our cosmos.
